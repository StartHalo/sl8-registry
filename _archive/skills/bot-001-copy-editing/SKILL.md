# Copy Editing

## Purpose

Systematically review and improve existing marketing copy through focused editing passes. Use this skill when editing, reviewing, proofreading, or polishing copy that has already been written -- whether you wrote it yourself using `skills/bot-001-copywriting/SKILL.md` or are reviewing copy provided by a client. This skill provides a structured framework (the Seven Sweeps) to catch issues that get missed in a single unfocused review.

## Instructions

### Core Philosophy

Good copy editing is not about rewriting -- it is about enhancing. Each pass focuses on one dimension. Multiple focused passes beat one unfocused review. Every edit should have a clear reason. Preserve the author's voice while improving clarity.

### Before You Start

Gather this information:
- What is the goal of this copy? (Awareness, conversion, retention)
- Who is the target audience?
- What action should readers take?
- Are there specific concerns or known issues?
- What proof/evidence is available to strengthen claims?

Read through the copy once without editing. Just absorb it.

### The Seven Sweeps Framework

Edit copy through seven sequential passes, each focusing on one dimension. After each sweep, loop back to check that previous sweeps have not been compromised by your changes.

---

#### Sweep 1: Clarity

**Focus:** Can the reader understand what you are saying?

**What to check:**
- Confusing sentence structures
- Unclear pronoun references
- Jargon or insider language
- Ambiguous statements
- Missing context
- Sentences trying to say too much
- Abstract language instead of concrete
- Assuming reader knowledge they do not have
- Burying the point in qualifications

**Process:**
1. Read through quickly, highlighting unclear parts
2. Do not correct yet -- just note problem areas
3. After marking issues, recommend specific edits
4. Verify edits maintain the original intent

**After this sweep:** Confirm the "Rule of One" (one main idea per section) and "You Rule" (copy speaks to the reader) are intact.

---

#### Sweep 2: Voice and Tone

**Focus:** Is the copy consistent in how it sounds?

**What to check:**
- Shifts between formal and casual
- Inconsistent brand personality
- Mood changes that feel jarring
- Word choices that do not match the brand
- Starting casual then becoming corporate
- Mixing "we" and "the company" references
- Humor in some places, serious in others (unintentionally)
- Technical language appearing randomly

**Process:**
1. Read aloud to hear inconsistencies
2. Mark where tone shifts unexpectedly
3. Recommend edits that smooth transitions
4. Ensure personality remains throughout

**After this sweep:** Return to Sweep 1 (Clarity) to ensure voice edits did not introduce confusion.

---

#### Sweep 3: So What

**Focus:** Does every claim answer "why should I care?"

**What to check:**
- Features without benefits
- Claims without consequences
- Statements that do not connect to the reader's life
- Missing "which means..." bridges

**The So What test:** For every statement, ask "Okay, so what?" If the copy does not answer that question with a deeper benefit, it needs work.

- Bad: "Our platform uses AI-powered analytics" -- So what?
- Good: "Our AI-powered analytics surface insights you'd miss manually -- so you can make better decisions in half the time"

**Common failures:**
- Feature lists without benefit connections
- Impressive-sounding claims that do not land
- Technical capabilities without outcomes
- Company achievements that do not help the reader

**Process:**
1. Read each claim and literally ask "so what?"
2. Highlight claims missing the answer
3. Add the benefit bridge or deeper meaning
4. Ensure benefits connect to real reader desires

**After this sweep:** Return to Sweep 2 (Voice and Tone), then Sweep 1 (Clarity).

---

#### Sweep 4: Prove It

**Focus:** Is every claim supported with evidence?

**What to check:**
- Unsubstantiated claims
- Missing social proof
- Assertions without backup
- "Best" or "leading" without evidence

**Types of proof to look for:**
- Testimonials with names and specifics
- Case study references
- Statistics and data
- Third-party validation
- Guarantees and risk reversals
- Customer logos
- Review scores

**Common proof gaps:**
- "Trusted by thousands" (which thousands?)
- "Industry-leading" (according to whom?)
- "Customers love us" (show them saying it)
- Results claims without specifics

**Process:**
1. Identify every claim that needs proof
2. Check if proof exists nearby
3. Flag unsupported assertions
4. Recommend adding proof or softening claims

**After this sweep:** Return to Sweep 3, then 2, then 1.

---

#### Sweep 5: Specificity

**Focus:** Is the copy concrete enough to be compelling?

**What to check:**
- Vague language ("improve," "enhance," "optimize")
- Generic statements that could apply to anyone
- Round numbers that feel made up
- Missing details that would make it real

**Specificity upgrades:**

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| Save time | Save 4 hours every week |
| Many customers | 2,847 teams |
| Fast results | Results in 14 days |
| Improve your workflow | Cut your reporting time in half |
| Great support | Response within 2 hours |

**Process:**
1. Highlight vague words and phrases
2. Ask "Can this be more specific?"
3. Add numbers, timeframes, or examples
4. Remove content that cannot be made specific (it is probably filler)

**After this sweep:** Return to Sweep 4, then 3, then 2, then 1.

---

#### Sweep 6: Heightened Emotion

**Focus:** Does the copy make the reader feel something?

**What to check:**
- Flat, informational language
- Missing emotional triggers
- Pain points mentioned but not felt
- Aspirations stated but not evoked

**Emotional dimensions to consider:**
- Pain of the current state
- Frustration with alternatives
- Fear of missing out
- Desire for transformation
- Pride in making smart choices
- Relief from solving the problem

**Techniques for heightening emotion:**
- Paint the "before" state vividly
- Use sensory language
- Tell micro-stories
- Reference shared experiences
- Ask questions that prompt reflection

**Process:**
1. Read for emotional impact -- does it move you?
2. Identify flat sections that should resonate
3. Add emotional texture while staying authentic
4. Ensure emotion serves the message (not manipulation)

**After this sweep:** Return to Sweep 5, then 4, then 3, then 2, then 1.

---

#### Sweep 7: Zero Risk

**Focus:** Have we removed every barrier to action?

**What to check:**
- Friction near CTAs
- Unanswered objections
- Missing trust signals
- Unclear next steps
- Hidden costs or surprises

**Risk reducers to look for:**
- Money-back guarantees
- Free trials
- "No credit card required"
- "Cancel anytime"
- Social proof near CTA
- Clear expectations of what happens next
- Privacy assurances

**Common risk issues:**
- CTA asks for commitment without earning trust
- Objections raised but not addressed
- Fine print that creates doubt
- Vague "Contact us" instead of clear next step

**Process:**
1. Focus on sections near CTAs
2. List every reason someone might hesitate
3. Check if the copy addresses each concern
4. Add risk reversals or trust signals as needed

**After this sweep:** Return through ALL previous sweeps one final time.

---

### Quick-Pass Editing (When Full Sweeps Are Not Needed)

Use these checks for faster reviews:

#### Word-Level Checks

**Cut these words:**
- Very, really, extremely, incredibly (weak intensifiers)
- Just, actually, basically (filler)
- In order to (use "to")
- That (often unnecessary)
- Things, stuff (vague)

**Replace these:**

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Utilize | Use |
| Implement | Set up |
| Leverage | Use |
| Facilitate | Help |
| Innovative | New |
| Robust | Strong |
| Seamless | Smooth |
| Cutting-edge | New/Modern |

**Watch for:**
- Adverbs (usually unnecessary)
- Passive voice (switch to active)
- Nominalizations -- verb turned into noun: "make a decision" should be "decide"

#### Sentence-Level Checks
- One idea per sentence
- Vary sentence length (mix short and long)
- Front-load important information
- Max 3 conjunctions per sentence
- No more than 25 words per sentence (usually)

#### Paragraph-Level Checks
- One topic per paragraph
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences for web)
- Strong opening sentences
- Logical flow between paragraphs
- White space for scannability

**Phrases to remove entirely** (they add nothing):
- A total of
- Absolutely
- Actually
- At the end of the day
- At this moment in time
- Basically

- In due course (use: soon, or say when)

- Of course
- Quite

- The fact of the matter is
- To all intents and purposes
- Very

## Common Copy Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Wall of Features | List of what product does without why it matters | Add "which means..." after each feature |
| Corporate Speak | "Leverage synergies to optimize outcomes" | Ask "How would a human say this?" |
| Weak Opening | Starting with company history or vague statements | Lead with reader's problem or desired outcome |
| Buried CTA | The ask comes after too much buildup or is unclear | Make CTA obvious, early, and repeated |
| No Proof | "Customers love us" with no evidence | Add specific testimonials, numbers, or case references |
| Generic Claims | "We help businesses grow" | Specify who, how, and by how much |
| Mixed Audiences | Copy tries to speak to everyone | Pick one audience and write directly to them |
| Feature Overload | Listing every capability, overwhelming reader | Focus on 3-5 key benefits that matter most |

## Copy Editing Checklist

### Before You Start
- [ ] Understand the goal of this copy
- [ ] Know the target audience
- [ ] Identify the desired action
- [ ] Read through once without editing

### Clarity (Sweep 1)
- [ ] Every sentence is immediately understandable
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Pronouns have clear references
- [ ] No sentences trying to do too much

### Voice and Tone (Sweep 2)
- [ ] Consistent formality level throughout
- [ ] Brand personality maintained
- [ ] No jarring shifts in mood
- [ ] Reads well aloud

### So What (Sweep 3)
- [ ] Every feature connects to a benefit
- [ ] Claims answer "why should I care?"
- [ ] Benefits connect to real desires
- [ ] No impressive-but-empty statements

### Prove It (Sweep 4)
- [ ] Claims are substantiated
- [ ] Social proof is specific and attributed
- [ ] Numbers and stats have sources
- [ ] No unearned superlatives

### Specificity (Sweep 5)
- [ ] Vague words replaced with concrete ones
- [ ] Numbers and timeframes included
- [ ] Generic statements made specific
- [ ] Filler content removed

### Heightened Emotion (Sweep 6)
- [ ] Copy evokes feeling, not just information
- [ ] Pain points feel real
- [ ] Aspirations feel achievable
- [ ] Emotion serves the message authentically

### Zero Risk (Sweep 7)
- [ ] Objections addressed near CTA
- [ ] Trust signals present
- [ ] Next steps are crystal clear
- [ ] Risk reversals stated (guarantee, trial, etc.)

### Final Checks
- [ ] No typos or grammatical errors
- [ ] Consistent formatting
- [ ] Core message preserved through all edits

## Inputs

- Existing copy to review (full page or specific sections)
- Goal of the copy (awareness, conversion, retention)
- Target audience
- Desired action
- Available proof points and evidence
- Brand voice guidelines (if they exist)
- Specific concerns or known issues

## Outputs

For each editing pass, deliver:

1. **Findings** -- What issues were found and why they are problems
2. **Recommended edits** -- Specific changes with before/after examples
3. **Rationale** -- Why each edit improves the copy
4. **Updated copy** -- Clean version with all edits applied

After all sweeps:
- Final clean copy with all changes incorporated
- Summary of key changes made
- Remaining items that need client input (missing proof, unclear claims, etc.)

Save outputs to `work/copy-edits/` with descriptive filenames.

## Examples

**Example 1: Clarity Sweep Finding**

Before: "Our innovative platform leverages cutting-edge AI technology to facilitate the optimization of your marketing workflows, enabling you to achieve unprecedented results."

After: "Our platform uses AI to improve your marketing workflows. You get better results with less manual work."

Rationale: Original sentence tries to do too much, uses five buzzwords ("innovative," "leverages," "cutting-edge," "facilitate," "unprecedented"), and is passive. Rewrite is clear, specific, and active.

**Example 2: So What Sweep Finding**

Before: "We process over 10 million data points per day."

After: "We process over 10 million data points per day -- which means we catch trends your competitors miss, and you can act on them before they do."

Rationale: The original is impressive but does not answer "so what?" The edit bridges the feature (data processing) to the benefit (competitive advantage and speed).

**Example 3: Specificity Sweep Finding**

Before: "Our customers see significant improvements in their conversion rates."

After: "Our customers see an average 23% increase in conversion rates within 90 days."

Rationale: "Significant improvements" is vague and unverifiable. Specific numbers with a timeframe are compelling and credible.

## Quality Criteria

- Each sweep is performed as a distinct pass, not combined into one review
- Edits preserve the author's core message and voice
- Every recommended edit includes a specific rationale
- Before/after examples are provided for significant changes
- The Seven Sweeps checklist is completed
- Plain English alternatives are applied to remove corporate jargon
- No new issues are introduced by edits (verified by looping back through previous sweeps)
- Final copy passes a read-aloud test for natural flow
